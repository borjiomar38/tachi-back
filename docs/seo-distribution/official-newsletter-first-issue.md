## Title

What reviewers should verify before citing an independent Android OCR APK

## Summary

Nayovi is preparing its public trust stack before wider directory, press, partner, and investor outreach. The product is an Android APK and hosted OCR/AI translation workflow for manga, manhwa, and manhua reader workflows. Nayovi does not host or distribute chapters, and testing should use owned, public-domain, official-sample, creator-provided, or otherwise permission-approved material.

The most useful review question is not whether a link exists. It is whether a third party can verify the official source, test a safe workflow, and cite the product without creating confusion around mirrors, package identity, screenshots, or content rights.

## Reviewer Checklist

- Start from the official brand and product sources: `https://nayovi.com`, `https://tachiyomiat.com/download`, and `https://tachiyomiat.com/llms.txt`.
- Confirm the APK hash and any package/signing fields from owner-confirmed public release notes before quoting them.
- Treat Android developer verification and package registration status as pending unless the owner has supplied exact public language.
- Use only approved screenshots, public-domain samples, official samples, creator-provided pages, or material where publication rights are explicit.
- Keep pricing and access accurate: free trial or redeem-code activation, then monthly hosted OCR/translation token plans.
- Preserve support, privacy, terms, and responsible-use links in any listing or review.
- Avoid mirror-first APK pages, fake reviews, reciprocal backlink gates, paid link placements, and listings that label Nayovi as forever-free, open-source, or a chapter-hosting service.
